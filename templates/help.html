About the Platform

This platform is designed to assist in the preliminary screening for B-cell Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (B-ALL) by analyzing blood smear images. Using advanced deep learning algorithms, the platform categorizes blood smear images into four classes:

  • Benign (non-leukemic)
  • Early Pre-B
  • Pre-B
  • Pro-B

These categories help in the early detection and differentiation of leukemia types, facilitating timely medical intervention.

How to Use the Platform

  • Upload Image: Upload a clear image of a blood smear slide. Ensure that the image is in a compatible format (JPEG, PNG) and meets the platform's quality guidelines.
  • Analyze: Click "Analyze" to begin processing. The platform will analyze the image and provide results within a few seconds.
  • Interpret Results: Once processing is complete, results will display the category assigned to the image (e.g., Benign, Early Pre-B, Pre-B, or Pro-B).
  • Further Guidance: If the results indicate a potential malignancy, please consult a medical professional for a detailed diagnosis.

Understanding the Results

  • Benign: Indicates that no leukemia cells are detected in the uploaded sample.
  • Early Pre-B, Pre-B, Pro-B: These labels represent various stages of B-cell development associated with B-ALL. If any of these categories are detected, it is recommended to seek further medical advice.

FAQs

  • What does this tool provide? This tool offers preliminary insights by classifying blood smear images into specific categories relevant to leukemia. It does not replace professional diagnosis.
  • Is my data secure? Yes, any uploaded images are processed securely, and privacy protocols are in place to protect user information.
  • What if my image quality is low? Clear, high-resolution images are essential for accurate analysis. Please re-upload if image quality does not meet platform guidelines.
